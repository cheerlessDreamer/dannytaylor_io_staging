BeepOff — AI Receptionist

Side project · Role: Designer, developer, everything · Timeline: 2025–present

The Problem

Hair salons are busy places. Stylists start their day with gloved hands full of bleach chemicals and a phone that won't stop ringing. Built for a specific salon — Andrew and Maxine's — but the problem is universal: a single-branch salon with a few staff, a Phorest setup, and no dedicated receptionist. There are only two major salon software players, and Andrew and Maxine know scores of friends in the same situation.

My Approach

I started with Lovable to build a static landing page — just to test interest and get something out there. The initial version was generous with "coming soon!" messaging. When it came to building the actual product, I used Vapi for voice AI, ElevenLabs for voice synthesis, Twilio for telephony, n8n for workflow orchestration, and integrated with the Phorest salon management API.

It worked manually very well — I could instantiate a new assistant — but provisioning telephone numbers, generating system prompts, and managing configurations wasn't scalable. I showed it to Andrew during one of our regular meetings, just as a sidenote because I thought it was interesting. He immediately asked whether he could use it at the salon and mentioned that Phorest had an API.

I used n8n specifically because I could see what was happening in real-time during testing — which workflows were failing, which were working, the value of variables throughout the process. I used Claude to help iron out bugs as well.

The Solution

A full SaaS application built with Lovable where salon owners sign up, choose a plan, and go through a short onboarding: Phorest details, salon information like opening hours, initial greetings, and voice selection. A workflow then provisions a local telephone number from Twilio, which gets stored in the database. The salon can immediately call the number to test the service — hearing things like the salon's name, the caller's name (if Phorest is linked), and answering questions about opening hours and appointments.

Impact

Maxine describes BeepOff as simply "part of the salon" now. Callers know who the AI is. The stylists love having a less stressful start to their day since they don't have to deal with the phones for a couple of hours. One paying customer so far, with a network of similar salons ready to expand to — I've held off sharing wider because I'm still too critical of it.

What I Learned

Vendor API documentation quality varies wildly. Vapi's API changed frequently, meaning I had to manually find the latest docs and examples. Phorest's documentation was sparse. The gap between "works manually" and "works at scale" is where most of the real engineering happens — provisioning, error handling, edge cases. And showing a side project casually to the right person is sometimes how real products are born.
